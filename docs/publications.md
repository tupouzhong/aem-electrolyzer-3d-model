## Key Metrics from Literature

### Current Distribution Studies
- Local current density variation: ±15-40% from average
- Improved by electrode design and flow distribution

### Bubble Dynamics
- Bubble diameter: 0.5-3 mm (typical)
- Void fraction: 5-25% in electrode regions
- Impact on ohmic resistance: 10-30% increase

### Energy Consumption (State-of-Art)
- Specific Energy Consumption (SEC): 
  - Current lab cells: 48-55 kWh/kg H₂
  - Commercial units: 55-65 kWh/kg H₂
  - Theoretical minimum: 33.3 kWh/kg H₂

### Faraday Efficiency
- Alkaline electrolysis: 90-98%
- AEM electrolysis: 92-96%
- Higher with improved membranes

## Research Gaps (Opportunities for Novel Contributions)

Based on literature analysis, these areas need more research:

1. ✗ **Coupled 3D models**: Few studies combine all five physics simultaneously
2. ✗ **Bubble dynamics coupling**: Most models ignore bubble effects on transport
3. ✗ **Thermal management**: Limited work on temperature distribution optimization
4. ✗ **Parameter sensitivity**: Systematic studies across wide ranges
5. ✗ **Electrode heterogeneity**: Non-uniform catalytic activity effects
6. ✗ **Membrane water uptake**: Dynamic water transport during operation
7. ✗ **Scale-up validation**: Lab scale (cm²) vs industrial (m²)

**Your 3D multiphysics model can address gaps #1-3, making it highly publishable!**
